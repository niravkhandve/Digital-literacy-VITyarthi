Title: UPI Payment Scam Through Fake Customer Support

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, especially among students and online shoppers. This type of fraud usually involves tricking users into authorizing payments instead of receiving money.

In a typical scenario, a college student tries to sell a used phone online. A fraudster contacts them pretending to be a buyer and agrees to pay via UPI. The scammer then sends a “payment request” instead of transferring money and instructs the student to “enter the UPI PIN to receive payment.” Believing this, the student enters their PIN, unknowingly authorizing a payment from their own account.

In another variation, scammers pose as bank or app customer support executives. They ask users to install screen-sharing apps or click malicious links, gaining access to sensitive information like OTPs and UPI PINs.

Students, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payments are common targets because they may not fully understand how UPI transactions work.

The consequences can be severe, including immediate financial loss, unauthorized bank transactions, and potential identity theft. Victims may also face stress and difficulty recovering lost money.

This case highlights the importance of understanding that receiving money never requires entering a UPI PIN, and users must stay cautious while dealing with unknown contacts online.
